# Billing Dunning Policy

## Overview

This document explains what happens when a payment fails on your account, the notifications you will receive, and how to restore your service. Our goal is to minimize disruption while giving you every opportunity to resolve payment issues.

---

## Dunning Schedule Explanation

When a payment fails, we follow a structured retry and notification schedule. This process — known as "dunning" — gives you multiple opportunities to update your payment method before any service interruption occurs.

### The Retry Schedule

| Day | Action | Description |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| **Day 1 (Immediate)** | Payment Retry + Notification | We immediately attempt a second payment charge and send a "Payment Failed" email to your account address. |
| **Day 3** | Retry + Reminder | We attempt another payment charge and send a "Payment Reminder" email if the previous attempt failed. |
| **Day 7** | Retry + Final Notice | We make a final payment attempt and send a "Final Payment Notice" email warning that service may be suspended if payment is not resolved. |
| **Day 14** | Suspension | If all payment attempts have failed, your account is suspended until payment is resolved. |

Each payment attempt uses the payment method on file. If you update your payment method before a scheduled retry, the new method will be used.

---

## Email Notification Schedule

We send a series of email notifications at each stage of the dunning process. All emails are sent to the address associated with your account.

### 1. Payment Failed (Day 1)

**Subject**: Your payment could not be processed

**Content**: This email informs you that a payment has failed and requests that you update your payment method in your account billing settings. It includes a direct link to your billing page and details about the failed charge amount.

### 2. Payment Reminder (Day 3)

**Subject**: Reminder: Update your payment method to avoid service interruption

**Content**: This email reminds you that your account is past due and that we are attempting to retry your payment. It reiterates the steps to update your payment method and warns that service may be affected if payment is not resolved.

### 3. Final Payment Notice (Day 7)

**Subject**: Final notice: Your account will be suspended in 7 days

**Content**: This email serves as a final warning before suspension. It explains that all retry attempts have failed, that your account will be suspended in 7 days if payment is not resolved, and provides clear steps to restore your account by updating your payment method.

### 4. Account Suspended (Day 14)

**Subject**: Your account has been suspended

**Content**: This email confirms that your account has been suspended due to non-payment. It explains what suspension means (loss of access to premium features) and provides instructions for reactivating your account by resolving the outstanding balance.

---

## Grace Period Details

You have a **14-day grace period** from the date of the initial payment failure before your account is suspended. During this grace period:

- Your account remains fully active with all premium features.
- We will retry your payment up to 3 times (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7).
- You can update your payment method at any time, and the next retry will use the new method.
- You will receive email notifications at each stage (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7).

You do not need to contact support to update your payment method. Simply go to your billing settings and update your card or payment information. Once updated, we will immediately attempt a new charge.

---

## Suspension and Reactivation Process

### What Happens When Your Account Is Suspended

On Day 14, if all payment retries have failed:

1. **Access is restricted**: Your account is downgraded to the Free tier. You lose access to premium features associated with your paid plan.
2. **Data is preserved**: Your projects, settings, and data are not deleted. They are preserved in case you reactivate your account.
3. **You receive a suspension email**: This email confirms the suspension and provides clear instructions for reactivation.

### How to Reactivate Your Account

To restore your paid plan after suspension:

1. **Update your payment method**: Log in to your account and go to billing settings. Update your card or add a new payment method.
2. **Confirm the charge**: We will immediately attempt to charge your updated payment method for the outstanding balance.
3. **Access is restored**: Once the charge is successful, your paid plan is reactivated immediately and you regain full access to all premium features.

If you need help or cannot resolve the payment issue yourself, contact our support team at support@platform.com. We can assist with payment plan arrangements in exceptional circumstances.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why did my payment fail?

Common reasons include an expired card, insufficient funds, bank fraud detection blocks, or incorrect card details. Check with your bank or card issuer for details.

### Can I update my payment method while in the grace period?

Yes. Go to your account's billing settings and update your payment method at any time during the grace period. We will attempt a new charge immediately with the updated method.

### Will I lose my data if my account is suspended?

No. Your data, projects, and settings are preserved during suspension. They are not deleted. You will regain full access once your account is reactivated.

### What exactly is suspended when my account is downgraded?

When suspended, your account is temporarily downgraded to the Free tier. You lose access to premium features (e.g., higher usage limits, advanced tools, team collaboration) but retain your data and basic account functionality.

### Can I call support to update my payment method?

For security reasons, we cannot process payment information over phone or email. Please update your payment method directly in your account's billing settings. If you need assistance with the process, our support team can guide you through it.

### How many times will you retry my payment?

We retry up to 3 times: immediately (Day 1), on Day 3, and on Day 7. Each retry uses the payment method currently on file.

### What if I updated my card but the charge still failed?

If you updated your payment method and it still fails, contact your bank to confirm the card is authorized for online/international charges. You can also try a different card. If the issue persists, contact our support team for assistance.

### Does the grace period start from the billing date?

Yes. The 14-day grace period begins on the day the original payment fails (your billing date). All notifications and retries are calculated from that date.
